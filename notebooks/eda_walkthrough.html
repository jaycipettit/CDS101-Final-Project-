  summarise(

    sd_x   = sd(x),
    mean_y = mean(y),
    sd_y   = sd(y),

4 4. Histogram of x (EDA + Visualization Quality)

This plot addresses both EDA and Visualization Quality:

  • It reveals the distribution of x.
  • It uses appropriate labels and a clear title.
ggplot(data, aes(x = x)) +
  geom_histogram(bins = 15, color = "white") +
  labs(
    title = "Distribution of x",
    x = "x",
    y = "Count"
  )

Histogram of x (EDA rubric: distribution of a key numeric variable).

Interpretation (example):

  • Comment on the shape (e.g., roughly symmetric, skewed, etc.).
  • Note any potential outliers.

You would tie this back to your research question, e.g.:

â€œIf we use x as a predictor, its roughly normal distribution is convenient for certain models.â€

5 5. Boxplot of y by Group (EDA: Comparing Groups)

This visualization supports:

  • Comparing distributions between groups (EDA).
  • Showing a clear, labeled plot (Visualization Quality).
ggplot(data, aes(x = group, y = y)) +
  geom_boxplot() +
  labs(
    title = "Distribution of y by Group",
    x = "Group",
    y = "y"
  )

Boxplot of y by group (EDA rubric: group comparison).

Interpretation (example):

  • How do medians compare between A and B?
  • Which group appears more variable?
  • Are there visible outliers?

You might relate this to a question such as:

â€œDoes group membership (A vs B) appear to influence y?â€

6 6. Scatter Plot of x vs y (EDA: Relationships)

This addresses EDA and prepares for Modeling Approach by visualizing relationships between variables.

ggplot(data, aes(x = x, y = y, color = group)) +
  geom_point(alpha = 0.7) +
  labs(
    title = "Relationship Between x and y by Group",
    x = "x",
    y = "y"
  )

Scatter plot of y versus x, colored by group (EDA rubric: relationship between variables).

Interpretation (example):

  • Describe the direction and strength of the relationship (e.g., roughly linear and positive).
  • Note whether points for groups A and B occupy different regions (interaction possibility).

This directly motivates modeling choices:

â€œBecause y increases with x and the relationship may differ by group, we might fit a regression model with an interaction term between x and group.â€
